What is Accounting?

Accounting simply means

Recording every single rupee that enters or leaves the business.

Example

You receive ₹20,000 from client.

Accounting says

Cash increased
Revenue increased

You paid hosting ₹700.

Accounting says

Cash decreased
Expense increased

Nothing more.

Every transaction is recorded forever.

⸻

Finance is different

Finance answers questions like

* How much money do we have?
* Can we hire one employee?
* Can we buy MacBook?
* Can we survive for 6 months?
* Can we give salary?

Accounting records.

Finance plans.

Looking at your current screen

Your Master Ledger is a strong starting point, but it mixes everything together. Instead of manually entering many items, your ERP should generate ledger entries automatically from other modules:

* Creating an invoice should automatically create the receivable.
* Recording a payment should automatically update the ledger and bank balance.
* Adding an expense should automatically reduce cash/bank and appear in the P&L.
* Processing payroll should automatically create salary expenses and payment entries.

That way, the ledger becomes the single source of truth, while users work in specialized modules.

For a company like Pixels Digital Solutions (agency + SaaS), building it this way will give you an ERP that can scale from your current business to dozens or even hundreds of clients without redesigning the accounting foundation later.
